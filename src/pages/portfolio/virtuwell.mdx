At Virtuwell, I led an 8-month project migrating the marketing site from static HTML to Contentful, in addition to broader front-end modernization and accessibility work across the platform.

## Headless CMS Migration

I led a four-person team — a BA, a designer, another developer, and myself — through migrating Virtuwell's marketing pages off static HTML and onto Contentful as a headless CMS. That meant setting the architecture, building the front-end to consume Contentful's API, reviewing the team's code, and working directly with stakeholders to plan the rollout.

Alongside the migration, I built an in-house design and component system for the marketing team to build pages with. It went through its own independent QA pass, separate from any individual page, which meant every page assembled from it started from a higher baseline of confidence than one-off hand-built markup ever could.

The result: publishing went from a monthly cycle to instantaneous. Content changes that used to require a developer to write code and then go through QA now ship the moment the marketing team hits publish, freeing up dev time that used to go entirely into content requests.

## NGRX Removal

I also removed NGRX from a large portion of one of Virtuwell's applications, which cut load times and made the codebase noticeably easier to maintain.

## Accessibility Improvements

Alongside that, I worked on accessibility improvements across Virtuwell's web applications — keyboard navigation, ARIA attributes, and color contrast fixes — to improve the experience for users with disabilities.

That mix of leading the CMS migration, cutting technical debt, and pushing accessibility forward is representative of what my time at Virtuwell looked like.
